Three laws of robotics

Rules developed by science-fiction writer Isaac Asimov, who
sought to create an ethical system for humans and robots

1st Law

Robot can not hurt a human being or through inaction allow, a human being to come to harm.

2nd Law

A robot must obey the order given it by human beings except where such order would conflict with the First law.

3rd Law

A robot must protect it's own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second law

Asimov later added another rule known as fourth or zeroth law, that superseded the others. It stated that "a robot may not harm humanity or by inaction allow humanity to come to harm".
